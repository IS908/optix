Recommendation Engine — 5-Step Logic

  1. 1
    IV Environment

    IV Rank ≥ 50 → high-IV environment, sell-side strategies have edge (IV expected to mean-revert downward, benefiting premium sellers).
    IV Rank < 30 → output Wait (Low IV), premiums insufficient to justify the risk.

  2. 2
    Directional Bias

    Weighted trend score (see Dashboard → Trend) determines strategy type: bullish → Sell Put / Bull Put Spread · bearish → Sell Call / Bear Call Spread · neutral → Iron Condor / Short Strangle. Max Pain vs current price and PCR extremes provide secondary adjustments.

  3. 3
    Strike Selection

    Short leg chosen at the intersection of: (a) nearest strong S/R level in the favourable direction, (b) large OI cluster acting as a natural wall, (c) target delta −0.20 to −0.30 for puts / +0.20 to +0.30 for calls (~70–80% OTM probability). The best strike is one where all three criteria align.

  4. 4
    Scoring & Filtering

    Each candidate strategy is scored:

    score = prob_OTM × 0.30
    + risk_reward_ratio × 0.25
    + theta_daily × 0.20 // daily Θ income, normalised
    + iv_rank × 0.15
    + breakeven_dist × 0.10 // % gap to B/E point

    Minimum thresholds (moderate risk): prob_OTM ≥ 60% · risk_reward ≥ 0.20 · margin ≤ 20% of capital.

  5. 5
    Top-3 Output

    Remaining candidates are sorted by score descending. The top 3 are shown with full details: expiry, strike, premium, max profit/loss, P&L ratio, margin required, net Greeks (Δ Θ ν), win probability, breakeven, and any risk warnings (e.g. earnings inside expiry).

Greeks Quick Reference

Greek Measures What to look for (sell-side perspective)
Δ DeltaPrice sensitivity ($ per +$1)Net delta near zero = market-neutral. For sell puts: negative delta, target −0.20 to −0.30.
Γ GammaDelta accelerationShort gamma (negative) for premium sellers — profits are capped but losses accelerate near the strike. Highest near ATM and near expiry.
Θ ThetaTime decay ($ per day)Positive theta = time works in your favour. Accelerates in the final 30 DTE — the sweet spot for premium sellers.
ν VegaIV sensitivity ($ per +1% IV)Negative vega for short premium — you profit when IV drops (IV crush after earnings, VIX spike resolved). High IV Rank environments maximise this edge.
ρ RhoInterest rate sensitivityMinor factor for short-dated options (<60 DTE). Positive for long calls, negative for long puts.

Data Sources & Caching

📡 Live mode (?refresh=true)
Fetches real-time quotes, OHLCV bars, and option chains directly from IB TWS via the IBAPI. The Python engine then runs full analysis in-process. Results are saved to the SQLite analysis cache for future cache-mode reads.
📦 Cache mode (default)
Reads the most-recent watchlist_snapshots row per symbol (populated by optix dashboard) and the full JSON payload from analysis_cache (populated by optix analyze or a previous live web request). No IB connection required.

IB TWS must be running and accepting API connections on the configured host/port for live mode to work. Paper trading (port 7497) and live trading (port 7496) are both supported.
